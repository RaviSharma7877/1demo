Advantages to Studying in Canada

Studying in Canada offers numerous advantages for international students

Quality Education

Canadian universities and colleges are known for their high academic standards, modern facilities, and innovative teaching methods. The country has a strong reputation for providing quality education, and many of its universities consistently rank among the top in the world.

Affordable Tuition

Compared to other popular study abroad destinations such as the United States, Canada offers affordable tuition fees for international students. Additionally, many Canadian universities offer scholarships and other financial aid programs to help offset the cost of tuition. Comparatively it is easier to get a Canadian student VISA.

Safe and Welcoming Environment

Canada is known for being a safe and welcoming country. The government has a strong commitment to human rights, equality, and diversity, and this is reflected in the country's multicultural population.

Access to Work Opportunities

International students in Canada are allowed to work while studying, giving them the opportunity to gain valuable work experience and earn money to support themselves. After graduation, students can also apply for a work VISA to stay and work in Canada.

Beautiful and Diverse Country

Canada is a vast country with stunning natural scenery, diverse cities, and a rich cultural heritage. International students have the opportunity to explore the country and immerse themselves in its unique culture.

Pathway to Permanent Residency

Canada has a number of immigration programs that offer a pathway to permanent residency for international students. After completing their studies, students can apply for a work permit and gain Canadian VISA for work, which can increase their chances of being invited to apply for permanent residency.

Top Universities To Study In Canada

These universities offer a wide range of programs and opportunities for students, and are recognized for their excellence in teaching and research.

University of Alberta

The University of Alberta (also known as U of A or UAlberta) is a public research university located in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. It was founded in 1908 by Alexander Cameron Rutherford, the first premier of Alberta, and Henry Marshall Tory, the university's first president. It was enabled through the Post-secondary Learning Act. The university is considered a "comprehensive academic and research university" (CARU), which means that it offers a range of academic and professional programs that generally lead to undergraduate and graduate level credentials. It is ranked among the top public universities in Canada by major college and university rankings.

University of British Columbia

With campuses in Vancouver and Kelowna, the University of British Columbia is a globally recognized institution that offers exceptional programs in fields such as forestry, computer science, and psychology.

University of Toronto

Ranked as the top university in Canada, the University of Toronto is known for its research excellence and innovative programs in a wide range of disciplines, including business, engineering, and the arts.

University of Waterloo

Located in Ontario, the University of Waterloo is a top-ranked institution that is known for its strengths in engineering, computer science, and mathematics. The university also has a strong focus on entrepreneurship and innovation.

University of Calgary

Located in Alberta, the University of Calgary is a research-intensive institution that offers a wide range of programs in fields such as engineering, business, and the humanities. The university is also known for its strong ties to industry and entrepreneurship.

Simon Fraser University

Located in British Columbia, Simon Fraser University is a top-ranked institution that offers innovative programs in fields such as communication, business, and the arts. The university is also known for its commitment to sustainability and social responsibility.

Intakes Available in Canada

Most Canadian universities have two main intakes: Fall (September) and Winter (January), with some offering a Summer (May) intake as well.

Fall Intake September to December

The Fall intake is the most popular and competitive intake period in Canada. It is the beginning of the academic year, and most universities offer a wide range of programs and courses during this intake. The Fall intake is ideal for students who want to start their studies right after completing their secondary school education or for those who prefer to begin their studies in September. Students who apply for the Fall intake may have access to more scholarships and financial aid opportunities than those who apply for other intake periods. Even the Canada Student VISA is easier to get during these time with the help of Canada student VISA consultant

Winter Intake January to April

The Winter intake is the second-most popular intake period in Canada. It offers a great opportunity for students who miss the Fall intake deadline or who want to take a break before starting their studies. The Winter intake period is also suitable for students who prefer a quieter and more relaxed academic environment. Many universities offer limited programs during the Winter intake period, so it's important to check the availability of the program you're interested in.

Summer Intake May to August

The Summer intake is the smallest and least popular intake period in Canada. It is suitable for students who want to study during the summer break or for those who want to complete a short-term program. The Summer intake period may be more flexible than the Fall and Winter intake periods, as some universities offer online or part-time courses during this period. However, not all programs are available during the Summer intake, and financial aid and scholarship opportunities may be limited. Students should also keep in mind that the Summer intake may affect their ability to work and study in Canada after graduation.

FAQ’S

Does Canada accept a 3-year Indian Degree?

Yes, for postgraduate (PG) diploma programs, Canadian institutions recognise the 10+2+3 educational system. However, for master's programs, practically all Canadian universities demand the 10+2+4 system.

Although, there are many universities in Canada accepting 3 years bachelor's degree. So, it is advisable to contact the universities for more details of their eligibility criteria.

Can international students work while studying in Canada?

You are eligible to work up to 20 hours per week but working more than that is a violation of your study permit conditions and may result in severe consequences like losing your student status for doing this, and may not be approved for a study or work permit in the future or you may also have to leave the country.

What English proficiency tests are recognised by Canadian universities?

There are two current authorized test providers for Canadian immigration for international students in English, the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) and the Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program (CELPIP).

Are Canadian degrees recognised internationally?

Yes, degrees from Canadian institutions are accepted worldwide and are known for their quality of education. For instance, they are recognised in various Commonwealth countries such as the UK, Australia, South Africa and New Zealand.

Is it possible to get a scholarship in Canada as an international student?

Global Affairs Canada is in charge of the Canadian government's participation in major international scholarship programmes. Each college has its own set of criteria for awarding international students scholarships.

What types of accommodation options are available in Canada for students?

As a student, you have to choose between on-campus and off-campus private housing accommodation. Some of the options available are duplexes, flats, residence halls, townhouses, condos, rental rooms and lofts.
