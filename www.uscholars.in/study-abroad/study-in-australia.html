Intakes Available in Australia

International students planning to study in Australia should be aware of the two main university enrolment deadlines. By planning ahead and knowing the timetable for commencing your studies, you can successfully apply and enrol for your desired courses.

Some universities offer additional intakes during the year, such as trimester or quarter systems, depending on the university and programme. It's important to check with your chosen university about the exact dates and deadlines for the start of your studies.

February/March Intake (September to February)

This is the first Intake of the year and the main Intake for most universities. The application period for this intake usually starts in August or September of the previous year and ends in January or February. Classes normally start in late February or early March.

July Intake (March to May)

This is the second and smaller Intake of the year. The application period for this cohort normally starts in March or April and ends in May or June. Classes usually start in late July or early August.

FAQS

What are the requirements for studying in Australia for international students?
To study as an international student in Australia, you must meet the following requirements:
  1. Enroll in an approved course.
  2. Prove your command of the English language.
  3. Get a student visa.
  4. Have sufficient health insurance coverage
  5. Show that you have sufficient financial resources.
  6. Meet the course's academic criteria.
  7. Meet any course age requirements.
It should be noted that these prerequisites may differ based on the institution and course, therefore check with the institution for specifics.
How can I apply for an Australian Student Visa?
  1. Choose Your Course and Institution: Select a course and institution that is registered with the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS).
  2. Receive an Enrollment Offer: Once you have been accepted into a CRICOS-registered course, you will receive an offer of enrollment from the institution.
  3. Gather Your Documents: You will need to gather your passport, offer of enrollment, proof of English language proficiency, health insurance, and financial documents.
  4. Create an Account and Apply Online: Create an account on the Department of Home Affairs website and complete the online application for a student visa.
  5. Pay the Visa Application Fee: Pay the visa application fee, which can vary depending on the type of visa and your country of origin.
  6. Attend a Biometric Appointment: You will be required to attend a biometric appointment to provide your fingerprints and photograph.
  7. Wait for a Decision: When you've submitted your application, you will need to wait for a decision. This can take several weeks or months.
What are the English language requirements for studying in Australia?
To study in Australia, international students are required to demonstrate English language proficiency. The most common ways to demonstrate English language proficiency are by taking one of the following tests:
  1. IELTS (International English Language Testing System): The minimum score required for admission to most courses is an overall band score of 6.5, with no individual band less than 6.0.
  2. TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language): The minimum score required for admission to most courses is 79 on the internet-based test, with a minimum of 19 in each section.
  3. PTE Academic (Pearson Test of English Academic): The minimum score required for admission to most courses is an overall score of 58, with no communicative skills score less than 50.
  4. Cambridge English Advanced (CAE): The minimum score required for admission to most courses is an overall score of 176, with no individual band less than 169.
It's important to note that the requirement for English language may vary depending on the institution and course you are applying to, so it's recommended that you check with the institution for specific requirements. Additionally, some institutions may offer English language courses to help students meet the language requirements.
Can I work while studying in Australia?
Yes, international students can work and support themselves while studying in Australia with a valid student visa. Generally, most visas allow work up to 40 hours a week during the academic year and full-time during scheduled course breaks. There are many part-time work opportunities available, and the minimum wage is relatively high. However, it's important to balance work with your studies and check visa requirements for specific work limitations.

How much does it cost to study in Australia?
The cost of studying in Australia can vary and depend on the institution, course, and location. However, international students can expect to pay tuition fees ranging from AUD$20,000 to AUD$45,000 per year for most undergraduate courses. Postgraduate courses can be more expensive. Additionally, you will need a proper budget for living expenses, including accommodation, food, transportation, and other costs.
Are there scholarships available for international students in Australia?
Yes, there are many scholarships available for international students in Australia. Some scholarships are offered by the Australian government, while others are offered by institutions, private organizations, and other sources. It's important to know well, research and apply for scholarships early, as competition can be high.
Can I stay in Australia after I finish my studies?
Yes, there are various visa options that are available for international students who want to stay in Australia after completing their studies. The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) allows you to stay in Australia and work after completing your studies for a period of up to 4 years, depending on your qualifications. You may also be eligible for other visa options, such as the Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189) or the Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190), if you meet all the eligibility requirements.
What are the types of student visas in Australia?
There are several types of student visas available for students who want to study in Australia:
  1. Student Visa (subclass 500): This is the most common type of student visa which allows you to study full-time in a registered course at an Australian institution. This visa is valid for the duration of your course and allows you to work full-time and up to 40 hours per week during the academic year scheduled course breaks.
  2. Student Guardian Visa (subclass 590): This visa is for the parents or legal guardians of international students who are under 18 years of age and studying in Australia on a student visa.
  3. Training Visa (subclass 407): This visa is for people who wish to come to Australia to undertake workplace-based training or participate in a professional development program.
  4. Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485): This visa allows international students who have recently graduated from an Australian institution to continue to stay in Australia and work for a period of up to 4 years, depending on their qualifications.
  5. Student Visa (subclass 590): This visa is for international students who want to undertake a non-award course or a course of study or training that does not lead to a formal Australian qualification.
It's important to note that each visa has its own requirements and eligibility criteria, and it is necessary to research and apply for the appropriate visa for your situation.
