Intakes Available in The USA

Universities in the USA typically have two main intakes, Fall and spring, with the Fall intake being the most popular. The fall intake usually starts in August/September, while the spring intake starts in January/February and the summer intake starts in May.

Fall Intake September to December

This is the main intake for most universities in the USA. It typically starts in August or September and runs until December or January. The Fall semester offers the widest range of courses, and most students begin their studies during this semester.

Winter Intake January to May

The Spring semester starts in January or February and runs until May or June. This intake is less common than the Fall semester, but it offers a great opportunity for students who miss the Fall semester deadline or who want to start their studies later in the year.

Spring Intake May to August

The Summer semester typically starts in May or June and runs until August or September. This intake is less common than the Fall and Spring semesters, but it offers students the chance to take courses over the summer months and potentially graduate earlier. It is also a good option for students who want to study during the summer break or who have other commitments during the traditional academic year.

FAQ’S

What are the types of student visas in the USA?
There are three main types of student visas available for students who wish to study in the United States:
  • F-1 Student Visa: The purpose of this visa category is to allow full-time students who desire to study at a US college or university, high school, private elementary school, seminary, conservatory, or any other recognised academic institution or language training program to apply for it.
  • J-1 Exchange Visitor Visa: This visa type is for exchange students who participate in approved programs for the purpose of teaching, instructing, or lecturing or for conducting research, observing, or consulting at research institutions, museums, libraries, or similar types of institutions.
  • M-1 Non-Academic Student Visa: This visa category is intended for individuals who want to undergo non-academic or vocational training, excluding language training programs, at institutions within the United States.
What is the best time to apply for a visa as a student in the US?
It is recommended to apply for a student visa to the US as early as possible, once you have received your I-20 or DS-2019 form from the university or institution you plan to attend. You can apply for a student visa up to 120 days before the start date of your program, but it is generally advised to apply well in advance to allow sufficient time for processing and any unexpected delays.
Is a 3-year Bachelor’s degree accepted in the USA?
Usually, universities in the US accept 4-year degree programs according to STEM but now many universities accept applicants with 3-year bachelor's degrees as well. Before applying, it is advisable to confirm with your selected university and school in the USA. And for those who don't, WES might be the key to opening doors to these universities for you.
Can international students work while studying in the US?
International students can work in the US with certain restrictions. They are allowed to work on-campus for up to 20 hours per week during term time and up to 40 hours per week during breaks. They can also work off-campus through Optional Practical Training (OPT) or Curricular Practical Training (CPT) which require work authorization. International students cannot work for employers in certain industries like politics or airlines. It's important for students to know their rights and restrictions and to consult their school officials for guidance.
Which English proficiency test is accepted in the US?
The English proficiency tests accepted in the US are the Cambridge Assessment English (CAE), the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) and the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL).
Are US degrees recognised internationally?
Most international firms accept degrees earned at US colleges and universities because of their academic brilliance and improved learning opportunities.
Is it possible to get a scholarship in the US as an international student?
There are several external scholarship programs available to international students, such as the Fulbright Scholarship, the Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship Program, and the Rotary Peace Fellowship, among others. These programs typically have their own application requirements and deadlines and can provide significant financial support for international students.
What types of student accommodation options are available in the US?
There are various types of student accommodation available in the US like residence halls, shared apartments, homestays and student dorms.
What is COE in the US visa application process?
The COE can refer to the Consular Electronic Application Center (CEAC) Confirmation of Enrollment, which is a document used to confirm enrollment in a US academic institution as part of the student visa application process.
Is there any limitation on the number of courses a student can pursue in the US per semester?
Foreign students are usually not limited in the number of courses they can take in the United States. However, they must maintain full-time enrollment status, which typically means enrolling in at least 12 credit hours per semester for undergraduate students and 9 credit hours per semester for graduate students.
What should an international student do for an extension of his/her Student Visa in the US?
If an international student needs to extend their student visa in the US, they should first check eligibility for visa extension and then should request an updated I-20 form from their designated school official (DSO) that includes a program extension recommendation. Lastly, The student should file Form I-539 (Application to Extend/Change Nonimmigrant Status) with the US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), along with all required documents and fees.
