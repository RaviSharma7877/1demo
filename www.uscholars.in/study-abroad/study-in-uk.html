Scholarships To Study in The United Kingdom

The universities in the UK are renowned for the high quality of education they offer, but as we all know, high quality comes at a high cost, which not all of us can afford. This is where scholarships come in, UK scholarships provide students with invaluable opportunities to pursue their academic goals at one of the world's best education destinations. The United Kingdom is known for its top universities and varied selection of study programmes, offers several scholarships to both domestic and international students. These scholarships seek to encourage and reward exceptional individuals who have demonstrated academic distinction, leadership potential, and a desire to make a meaningful contribution in their chosen professions. UK scholarships enable individuals to realise their full potential and contribute to society on a global scale by offering financial support and access to world-class education. Here are the top 5 scholarships for international students.

Chevening Scholarship

The UK government sponsors the Chevening Scholarships, which are given to deserving candidates from all across the world. For students pursuing a master's degree at any UK university, these scholarships include a travel allowance, complete tuition coverage, and living expenses.

Rhodes Scholarship

The oldest and most renowned grant is the Rhodes scholarship. The scholarship, which was established in 1902 as a result of Cecil Rhodes's bequest, aims to recognise and assist people who demonstrate academic achievement, leadership potential, and a dedication to having a beneficial impact on the world. In addition to receiving complete funding for their studies, including tuition costs, living expenses, and additional support for research and travel, Rhodes Scholars are chosen from a variety of nations across the world.

  • Study in: UK
  • Study Level: Post Graduate

Commonwealth Scholarship

The Commonwealth Scholarship is a prominent programme that is funded and also supported by the Commonwealth Countries, It gives students from Commonwealth nations the chance to pursue postgraduate education in the UK. Scholarships are given out in accordance with academic achievement and the likelihood of having a good effect on the applicant's native nation. The full financing for tuition, living costs, and travel expenses provided by Commonwealth Scholarships allows students to fully concentrate on their coursework and research. 

  • Institution: Various Universities in the UK

Erasmus Mundus Scholarship

The Erasmus Mundus is an elite scholarship programme which offers various opportunities to students from all around the world  the chance to enrol in combined programmes at premier universities in Europe, including several in the UK. The scholarship, which is supported by the European Union. The Erasmus Mundus Scholarships pay for tuition, offer a stipend for living costs each month, and offer a travel allowance to allow students to move between member universities.

Cost of Living

The factors that determine the cost of living in the UK include location, lifestyle decisions, and personal preferences, to name just a few. In general, major cities like London are more expensive than other places. Below is an estimation of the cost of living in the UK:

Accommodations

£ 80 - £ 320

Depending on the city and type of lodging, rent costs can vary widely. For instance, a one-bedroom flat in central London can cost between £1,500 and £3,000 per month, while those outside the city centre can cost between £1,200 and £2,000. Rent may be less expensive in other places, with one-bedroom apartments costing between £800 and £1,500 per month.

Utilities

£ 100 - £ 500

Depending on the size of the flat and use, monthly utility bills, which include electricity, heating, cooling, and water can range from £100 to £500.

Food

£ 10 - £ 250

Depending on personal tastes, grocery costs can vary, but an approximate estimate for a single person's monthly food budget would be between £150 and £250. The cost of a lunch when eating out at restaurants or cafés can range from £10 to £30.

Transportation

£ 130 - £ 180

The cost of public transit will vary by city and frequency of use. For instance, a monthly transport card for unlimited travel within certain zones in London can run you anywhere from £130 to £180. Public transit may be less expensive in other cities.

Internet and Mobile Phone

£ 10 - £ 50

Count on paying between £30 and £50 a month for a broadband connection. Although there are many other mobile phone plans, a basic plan normally costs £10 to £30 per month.

Other Expenses

£ 10 - £ 100

Healthcare (if not provided by the National Health Service), entertainment, clothing, and personal care products could all incur additional costs. Depending on preferences and conditions, these costs may change.

Visa

Having a student visa in the UK allows overseas students to pursue their academic objectives and enjoy priceless educational experiences at renowned British institutions. With a student visa, you may access a top-notch education and a plethora of chances for both personal and professional development in the UK. The UK draws students from all over the world because of its prestigious universities and colleges. The student visa offers the means to pursue your academic goals, whether you want to conduct ground-breaking research, seek a degree in a specialised subject, or improve your language abilities.

General Tier 4 Student Visa

Cost - £ 363

Eligibility: English language proficiency and an offer of a spot on a course at a reputable educational institution are requirements for eligibility.

Documents: Required paperwork includes your institution's CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies), financial documentation, a current passport, and confirmation of English language proficiency.

Process: Apply online, fill out the visa application form, go to the biometric appointment, and provide the necessary paperwork.

Child Student Visa under Tier 4

Cost - £ 348

Eligibility: Ages 4 to 17 are eligible if they are offered a spot on a programme, have parental or guardian approval, and have acceptable housing.

Documents Needed: CAS, evidence of parental consent, financial documentation, a current passport, and information regarding lodging.

Process: Apply online, fill out the visa application form, go to the biometric appointment, and provide the necessary paperwork.

Visiting Student Visa

Cost - £ 200

Eligibility: Requirements include enrollment in a short course (up to six months) or English language programme, having sufficient funds, and planning to leave the UK after graduation.

Documents: Required paperwork includes a valid passport, financial records, proof of enrollment, and a vacation itinerary.

Process: Apply online, fill out the form for a visa, attach supporting documentation, and appear at the biometric appointment.

Intakes Available in The United Kingdom

The main intake for UK universities is in September/October for undergraduate and graduate courses, with some universities offering a second intake in January and April.

Autumn Intake September - October

The most common intake for international students, the Autumn term offers a chance to start at the beginning of the academic year and participate in the full range of activities and events offered by the university. It's also an ideal time to get settled in the UK, as the weather is still mild and there's plenty of time to adjust to the new environment.

Spring Intake January - February

The Spring term is a great option for students who missed the Autumn intake or need more time to prepare. It also offers the advantage of potentially lower competition for places and accommodation. However, it may be challenging to catch up with the coursework and settle into the university community, as the academic year is already in progress.

Summer Intake April- May

The Summer term is typically the shortest of the three and may offer limited course options. However, it can be a good option for students seeking to take advantage of summer programs, internships, or other activities. It's also a good time to enjoy the warmer weather and explore the UK's many cultural and natural attractions.

FAQ’S

What are the scholarship options available for students to study in the UK?
In the UK, there are numerous scholarship opportunities accessible for international students. Government scholarships, international scholarships, and university scholarships can all be categorised under them. Every scholarship has a different percentage of tuition paid, and some even partially cover the student's living expenses. Every scholarship has a different set of conditions, and each institution has its own. Eight to nine months before the intended intake begins, this process should be initiated.
Do institutions in the UK accept 3-year degrees?
Yes, institutions in the UK do accept 3-year degrees, as long as the degree is awarded by a recognized institution and meets the academic requirements for admission to the specific program or institution. In fact, the majority of undergraduate degrees in the UK are designed to be completed in three years, although some programs may take longer depending on the subject area and academic requirements.
What documents are required for students to apply for a UK visa?
The documents required for students to apply for a UK visa will depend on the specific type of visa they are applying for and their individual circumstances. However, generally, the following documents are required for a Tier 4 (General) Student visa application:
  • A valid passport or travel document
  • A recent photograph
  • A Certificate of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from a UK university or institution
  • Proof of English language proficiency (usually a valid IELTS or TOEFL score)
  • Evidence of sufficient funds to cover tuition fees and living expenses in the UK
  • Academic qualifications, including transcripts and certificates
  • Confirmation of any relevant work experience
  • Visa Application
It's important for students to check the specific requirements for their individual circumstances and type of visa, as well as any additional documents that may be required. Additionally, all documents must be in English or translated into English by a certified translator.
Can you work during completing your studies in the UK?
Yes, as an international student in the UK on a Tier 4 (General) Student visa, you are generally allowed to work part-time during your studies. The UK government allows Tier 4 visa holders to work for up to 20 hours per week during term-time and full-time during scheduled holidays.
What are the English language requirements to study in the UK?
In order to be granted a Student Route visa (previously known as a Tier 4 visa) to pursue undergraduate or graduate studies, every student who needs a visa to study in the UK must show proof of a minimum level of English language proficiency (Common European Framework (CEFR) B2 level).

You must also take The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) for UK Immigration and (UKVI), as it is the English language competency test that is most widely accepted by UK institutions and is also encouraged by UK immigration regulations. TOEFL and Duolingo are two additional exams.
Does the duration of a semester matter in getting a Visa in the UK?
Students pursuing a degree are permitted to stay in the UK for up to five years. (Students must be at least 18 years old.) Students can normally stay in the UK for up to two years if their course is not at the degree level. However, there are options for extending your visa. For further information or if you have any questions, please contact Best Student Halls.
What is the best time to apply for a Visa as a student?
Six months before the commencement of your course is the ideal time to submit an application for a student visa. Take note that you are only permitted to apply up to 3 months before the course start date listed on your CAS. The entire procedure, from admission to receiving your final visa, can typically take 30-45 working days. The processing of the student visa application takes 15 working days. Complex cases may take months to process, though this varies from case to case.
What is CAS and can it be used more than once?
CAS is an abbreviation for Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies. This is an electronic letter from the university regarding your student visa application. Your personal information, course of study, and unique CAS number are all included in the letter. A CAS number is required when applying for a Tier 4 UK visa. 

Because each visa application has a unique CAS number, you cannot use the same CAS number twice. The CAS numbers are valid for six months after they are issued. If your visa application is denied, you should contact us for further guidance.
What are the accommodation options available in the UK for students?
You have the choice of living on campus or finding off-campus housing as an international student. The majority of UK universities provide halls of residence for international students. On-campus accommodation is a very economical option, but you must apply separately in advance to secure a place for yourself.

If you are unable to find such housing or want to travel alone, you can rent a room in a shared flat or stay with a local British family. Keep in mind that rental prices will differ from city to city. We will also assist you in locating the best possible student accommodation.
