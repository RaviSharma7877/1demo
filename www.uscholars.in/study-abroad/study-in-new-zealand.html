FAQS

What is the application process for studying in New Zealand?
The application process for studying in New Zealand involves researching universities and courses, meeting admission requirements, submitting an online application with supporting documents, waiting for a decision, accepting an offer, applying for a student visa, and making travel arrangements.
What is the cost of studying in New Zealand?
The cost of studying in New Zealand varies depending on the course, university, and location. On average, tuition fees for international students range from NZD 22,000 to NZD 32,000 per year for undergraduate programs and NZD 26,000 to NZD 37,000 per year for postgraduate programs.
What are the types of student visas in New Zealand?
There are three types of student visas available for international students who want to study in New Zealand:
  • Fee Paying Student Visa: This visa is for students who have been accepted into a full-time course at a New Zealand educational institution and who have paid the tuition fees in advance. This visa allows students to stay in New Zealand for the duration of their course.
  • Exchange Student Visa: This visa is for students who are enrolled in an exchange program between their home institution and a New Zealand educational institution. This visa allows students to stay in New Zealand for up to 4 months.
  • Pathway Student Visa: This visa is for students who plan to take multiple courses in New Zealand. This visa allows students to stay in New Zealand for up to 5 years and is granted for the duration of the student's course of study.
Are Scholarships available for international students in New Zealand?
Yes, there are scholarships available for international students to study in New Zealand. Some of the popular scholarships include New Zealand Development Scholarships, New Zealand International Doctoral Research Scholarships, and New Zealand Excellence Awards.
What are the English Language requirements for International students in New Zealand?
International students who want to study in New Zealand are generally required to demonstrate their proficiency in English. The specific English language requirements may vary depending on the institution and the program of study, but here are some general guidelines:
  • TOEFL: The Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) is accepted by most universities in New Zealand. The minimum score required varies between institutions, but is typically between 80 and 100 for the internet-based test.
  • IELTS: The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is also widely accepted in New Zealand. The minimum score required varies between institutions, but is typically between 6.0 and 7.0 for the academic version of the test.
  • Other Tests: Some universities in New Zealand may accept other English language tests, such as Pearson Test of English (PTE), Cambridge English: Advanced (CAE), or Occupational English Test (OET).
  • Exemptions: International students may be exempt from English language requirements if they have completed their education in a country where English is the primary language or if they have completed a qualification in English.
Can International students work while studying in New Zealand?
Yes, international students in New Zealand are allowed to work part-time up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during the summer break. However, students must have a valid student visa and meet the eligibility criteria.
What should an international student do to extend their student visa in New Zealand?
To extend a student visa in New Zealand, international students should check the eligibility criteria and visa requirements on the Immigration New Zealand website, apply online, pay the fee, wait for a decision, and follow the visa conditions upon approval. It's important to start the process well in advance of the current visa expiration date.
What are the accommodation options for international students in New Zealand?
Accommodation options for international students in New Zealand include on-campus dormitories, homestays, private rentals, and shared apartments. Most universities also have a dedicated international student support team to assist students with their accommodation needs.
What is the job market like for international students in New Zealand?
The job market in New Zealand is competitive, but international students with relevant skills and qualifications can find employment opportunities in their field of study. The government also offers a post-study work visa that allows graduates to work in New Zealand for up to three years after completing their studies.
